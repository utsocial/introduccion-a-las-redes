Desarrollo de TCP/IP

La primera red de conmutación de paquetes, antecesora de Internet actual, fue la red Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET), que tuvo su origen en 1969 al conectar PC centrales en cuatro ubicaciones. ARPANET fue fundada por el Departamento de Defensa de los Estados Unidos para que se utilice en universidades y en laboratorios de investigación.

Figure is a timeline that shows the evolution of the Internet starting from 1965. A few highlights in time are listed. In 1969 the first messages were transmitted from the University of California Los Angeles Stanford Research Institute. In 1981 the TCP and IP protocols were formalized. In 1984, the Domain Name Service was introduced. In 1985 the file transfer protocol was documented. In 1986 Cisco launched its first routing innovation, which was a multiprotocol router. In 1993 the first web browser was developed by Mr. Marc Andreessen at the University of Illinois, Champaign-Urbana and was called MOSAIC. June 8 of two thousand eleven was the first World IP version 6 day.
